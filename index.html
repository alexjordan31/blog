How to Annoy People but Others to Take the Blame

By Alexander Iordanov | May 5, 2025

Look, I’m not saying you *should* annoy people and make others take the fall. But if you were hypothetically interested in such tactics—for academic reasons—then you’ve come to the right place.

Start small. Crank the thermostat to "tropical sauna" and then blame it on Grandma. Everyone loves her too much to argue. You? Completely off the hook, now comfortably sweating in smug satisfaction.

Or switch the TV remote batteries with old ones. When your housemate starts smacking it like it owes them rent, feign surprise and say, “Maybe it’s a ghost. Or Chad again…”

How to Look Busy Doing Absolutely Nothing

By Alexander Iordanov | April 30, 2025

Here’s the trick: furrow your brow, hold a pen, and mutter angrily. Nobody will question your productivity. Toss in an occasional deep sigh. Bonus points if you open a spreadsheet and stare at it like it's the Matrix.

My personal favorite? Carry a coffee cup and walk fast. Destination? Nowhere. Purpose? Nonexistent. But no one asks questions because you're clearly “on a mission.”

Incorporate mysterious head nods and the occasional “Can’t talk, deadline!” Boom. Untouchable. Invisible. Legendary.

How to Win Arguments by Pretending You Can't Hear

By Alexander Iordanov | April 22, 2025

Step one: tilt your head slightly and squint like you’re trying to tune into another dimension. Step two: respond with “Hmm? Must be this old ear of mine,” and walk away mid-sentence.

They can't argue if you’re not participating. That’s science. If they text you, respond 3 hours later with “Sorry, was watering the microwave.” They’ll give up. You’ll win by attrition.

In my house, we call this strategy "strategic auditory denial." Others call it "being a jerk." Potato, potahto.
